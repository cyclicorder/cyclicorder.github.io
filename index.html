Research focus
What I work on

My research sits at the intersection of representation geometry, adversarial machine learning, and multimodal AI safety. I study how vision-language models encode information — and crucially, how that encoding breaks down.

Current work targets adversarial vulnerabilities in BLIP-2, LLaVA-1.5, and Qwen-VL, probing teacher-forcing gaps, gradient dilution, and Q-Former bottleneck effects through a mechanistic interpretability lens.

Earlier work revealed surprising geometric properties of ViT embedding spaces: equivalence structures, inverted LID behavior, and cross-modal semantic misalignment — findings with direct implications for model safety and robustness evaluation.

Adversarial attacks on VLLMs
Teacher-forcing exploitation, gradient dilution, attention knockout in BLIP-2, LLaVA-1.5 & Qwen-VL
ViT embedding geometry
Equivalence structures, local Lipschitz estimation, intrinsic dimensionality (LID), Jacobian/SVD curvature
Mechanistic interpretability
Attention knockout, information flow analysis, cross-modal alignment failure in multimodal transformers
Representation vulnerability
Semantic misalignment, noise-voting detection, adversarial robustness evaluation in shared embedding spaces
